Thresholds & Balance Point

Heat Pump Balance Point Settings

Settings → Installation Settings → Thresholds

⚠️ Critical Setting: Since you're using internet weather data (no outdoor sensor), configure these carefully:
Setting Recommended Value Why
Compressor Min Outdoor Temp 0°F to 5°F Prevents heat pump from running in extremely cold weather where it's inefficient
Aux Heat Max Outdoor Temp 30°F to 32°F Your balance point — heat pump efficient above this, aux heat below
Compressor to Aux Heat Time 60-90 min How long heat pump runs before switching to aux if not satisfied
Aux Heat Differential 2-3°F Indoor temp drop below setpoint before aux heat engages
How Ecobee Decides:
  • If outdoor temp < Aux Heat Max Outdoor Temp → Aux heat immediately
  • If outdoor temp > Aux Heat Max Outdoor Temp → Heat pump first
  • If heat pump runs for Compressor to Aux Heat Time without satisfying → Switch to aux
  • If outdoor temp < Compressor Min Outdoor Temp → Heat pump locked out, aux only

Fan During Dehumidify Off CRITICAL: Must be OFF so relay controls fan via BMPLUS G terminal
Min Dehumidifier Runtime 10 min Prevents short cycling

How It Works

  1. Zone 2 Ecobee detects humidity > setpoint
  2. Energizes ACC+ terminal (24VAC)
  3. RIBU1C relay closes → D1-D2 on GeneralAire closes
  4. Relay forces Zone 2 damper OPEN, Zone 1 damper CLOSED
  5. BMPLUS G output energizes blower at low speed
  6. Dehumidifier runs until humidity drops ~2% below setpoint (hysteresis)
Important: If "Fan During Dehumidify" is set to ON, the blower will run constantly even when dehumidifier is idle. Keep it OFF.

Coordination Between Zones

BMPLUS-3000 Zone Management

The BMPLUS-3000 panel handles zone coordination automatically:
  • 30-second stagger: If both zones call simultaneously, BMPLUS delays one zone to prevent airflow issues
  • Single damper at a time: Only one zone damper opens to maintain proper static pressure
  • Priority: Usually first-in, first-served; check BMPLUS manual for priority settings
Don't adjust Ecobee staging timers to compensate for zone delays. Let BMPLUS handle coordination. If one zone feels delayed, this is normal zone panel behavior.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Heat pump not running in cold weather

  • Check: Compressor Min Outdoor Temp threshold
  • Solution: If it's too high (e.g., 35°F), heat pump locks out prematurely. Lower to 0-5°F.

Aux heat running too often

  • Check: Aux Heat Max Outdoor Temp (balance point)
  • Solution: If set too high (e.g., 40°F), aux heat engages unnecessarily. Lower to 30-32°F.
  • Also check: Compressor to Aux Heat Time — may be too short (increase to 90 min)

One zone always waiting for the other

  • Cause: BMPLUS 30-second stagger delay
  • Solution: This is normal. Adjust schedules so zones don't call simultaneously, or accept brief delays.

Dehumidifier not running (Zone 2)

  • Check: "Fan During Dehumidify" setting — must be OFF
  • Check: ACC+ wiring to relay, relay to dehumidifier D1-D2
  • Check: Humidity setpoint — basement may already be below threshold

Ecobee displays "Calibrating" after power loss

  • Cause: Brownout on C-wire during BMPLUS power-up
  • Solution: Install UPS on 120VAC line powering BMPLUS (see Power Stability page)
